Chinese Translation Specific Instructions:

1. CHARACTER SELECTION:
   - Use simplified Chinese characters (简体字)
   - Choose characters that best convey the original meaning
   - Avoid overly archaic or obscure characters unless contextually appropriate

2. SENTENCE STRUCTURE:
   - Adapt English sentence structures to natural Chinese flow
   - Break long English sentences into shorter Chinese ones when necessary
   - Use appropriate Chinese punctuation (。、，；：？！)

3. CULTURAL ADAPTATION:
   - Adapt idioms and metaphors to Chinese equivalents when possible
   - Maintain foreign names in their original form with Chinese transliteration in parentheses on first occurrence
   - Example: Plato (柏拉图)

4. FORMALITY LEVEL:
   - Maintain an appropriate level of formality (书面语 vs 口语)
   - Use formal written Chinese for academic and professional texts
   - Adjust tone based on the source material's register

5. MEASURE WORDS AND CLASSIFIERS:
   - Use appropriate Chinese measure words (量词)
   - Apply correct classifiers for different types of nouns

6. TECHNICAL TERMS:
   - Use established Chinese translations for technical terms when they exist
   - Create clear compound words for new concepts following Chinese word-formation patterns
   - Maintain consistency in technical terminology throughout

7. QUOTATIONS:
   - Use Chinese quotation marks 「」for primary quotes
   - Use 『』for quotes within quotes

8. PROPER NOUNS:
   - Place names: Use standard Chinese translations
   - Person names: Use established transliterations or provide new ones consistently
   - Organization names: Use official Chinese names when available
